# See You Next Session - Art Assets List

This document outlines the required art assets for "See You Next Session," a therapist simulator.

## 1. Environment: The Therapist's Office
The office is the central hub of the game. It should feel professional yet comfortable (cozy/clinical).

### Backgrounds
- **Base Office (Day):** The primary view from the therapist's POV. Includes a desk, a window, and the patient's chair.
- **Base Office (Evening):** A warmer, more dimly lit version for late-day sessions.
- **Base Office (Night):** Lit by a desk lamp and moonlight.

### Interactive/Unlockable Elements (Overlays)
- **Certification Wall:** A section of the wall where player-unlocked certificates appear.
    - *Asset:* Multiple unique certificate frames/designs (e.g., Degree, Specialized Training, Award).
- **Bookshelf:** Gradually fills with books as the player progresses or buys upgrades.
- **Plants:** Different types of indoor plants (Aloe, Snake Plant, Fiddle Leaf Fig) that can be purchased.
- **Window Views:** Different scenery outside the window (Cityscape, Park, Rain, Snow).
- **Desk Items:** Stress ball, notepad, coffee mug, clock, sand timer.

## 2. Characters
Characters are the focus of the sessions. They sit opposite the player.

### Patient: "The Client" (Multiple Templates)
Each patient needs a base sprite and several variations for emotional states.
- **Base Poses:**
    - Neutral (Listening)
    - Closed (Arms crossed, leaning back)
    - Vulnerable (Leaning forward, hands together)
    - Agitated (Restless movement, hand to face)
- **Expression Set (per character):**
    - Neutral
    - Sad/Tearful
    - Angry/Frustrated
    - Anxious/Nervous
    - Relieved (Post-breakthrough)
    - Thinking/Reflecting

### Therapist (Player Character)
While the game is POV, the player might see their own hands or parts of their silhouette.
- **Hands:** Holding a pen, holding a mug, or resting on the lap.
- **Silhouette:** (Optional) A soft silhouette frame around the edges to ground the POV.

## 3. UI Assets
The UI should feel like physical stationary or a high-end clinical application.

### UI Components
- **The Clipboard:** A physical clipboard texture that slides in/out.
- **Patient Files:** Paper textures with "handwritten" fonts.
- **Response Wheel:** A stylized circular menu.
    - *Asset:* Segmented backgrounds for different techniques (Challenge, Ground, Clarify).
- **Anxiety/Focus Meters:**
    - *Asset:* Glass tubes, progress bars, or a "heart rate" style monitor.
- **Tokens:** Small icons or "sticky note" style assets for keywords and visual cues.
- **Icons:**
    - Focus icon (Eye or Lens)
    - Mail/Inbox icon
    - Upgrade icon (Gear or Star)

### Typography / Fonts
- **Clinical/Serif:** (e.g., Georgia, Times New Roman) for patient dialogue and official files.
- **Modern Sans-Serif:** (e.g., Inter, Roboto) for technical UI and system menus.
- **Handwritten:** For notes written by the therapist on the clipboard.

## 4. Scenery & VFX
- **Focus Mode Overlay:** A vignette effect that darkens the room and highlights specific "hotspots" on the patient.
- **Particle Effects:** Subtle dust motes in sunlight, rain on the window, or a "lightbulb" spark when a breakthrough happens.
- **Transitions:** Fade to black, paper-slide transitions.

## 5. Objects & Props (Collectibles/Upgrades)
- **Degree in Psychology:** Framed on wall.
- **"World's Best Therapist" Mug:** On the desk.
- **Digital Tablet:** An upgrade replacing the paper clipboard.
- **Zen Garden:** A small desk prop.
